Overcoming Denial to Make a Change

Imagine discovering that your child that you truly love could be the one causing another child pain. Would you admit these signs to solve this behavior? or would fear and denial keep you from seeing the truth? This is a question to think of for a while. Do we care about others and seek to live in a safe community or do we just stand with falsity refusing to see the truth because we are selfish?

Parents’ denial of their children's mistakes, like bullying in our case, is a serious problem that professionals who solve bullying behavior struggle with. Not recognizing their unpleasant attitudes hinders the process of solving the problem and further pushes these children to continue bullying extensively because their parents support them in the wrong way. This problem stems from some psychological issues. However, recognizing these issues now and solving them is necessary for parents to not let their children destroy themselves with their detrimental attitudes while parents cannot help them now.

It’s natural for parents to want to protect their children, but true protection comes from facing uncomfortable truths, not hiding from them

"The first step toward change is awareness. The second step is acceptance."
--Psychologist Nathaniel Branden

Understanding the Consequences of Overlooking Bullying Behaviors

Overcoming parental denial is a vital step because acknowledging problems is the first step for real change to take place. For parents, it is natural that they want to see the best in their children, but sometimes this instinct can blind them from difficult truths like the possibility that their children may be engaging in bullying. Parental denial may stem from fear, shame, or a lack of awareness about the severity of bullying. Therefore, identifying these psychological barriers is essential for effective intervention (Everhart, 2023).​

These parents may worry about what others will think, feel guilty, or believe that “kids will be kids eventually”. However, Everhart (2023) argues that failure to acknowledge a child's bullying behavior can lead to an endless cycle of aggression, making it harder for children to build healthy relationships. This increases the risk of long-term emotional harm and social isolation, which leads to overall unsuccessful life experiences. Hence, recognizing the problem from the early beginning paves the way for effective intervention, guiding the child toward better behavior and helping them build healthier relationships.

Taking Action

But there is hope. When parents are open to acknowledging and discussing these issues, early intervention becomes possible, which reduces the overall incidence of bullying and guides children toward better behavior. Programs that encourage open, non-judgmental dialogue are especially effective in engaging parents and breaking down defensive barriers (Everhart, 2023).

Read More


Orientation in Egypt now is to adopt this strategy by making a non-threatening and non-judgemental community that acknowledges parents who recognize their children’s faults and seek to fix them. Makary (2021) highlights how Egypt’s Ministry of Education has launched workshops for parents, emphasizing the family’s role in promoting empathy and respect, proving that parental involvement is a main pillar of successful anti-bullying strategies.

Read More


Parental denial is a real problem that hinders our ability to truly address bullying. Solving this problem is a huge step toward better intervention. Professionals recommend treating defensive parents with empathy and without blame, and this shifts the focus on the behavior rather than the child’s character, preventing these parents from being offensive. Overall, this creates a safe and non-judgmental environment for these conversations and encourages honesty and collaboration, paving the way for meaningful change.
Let’s be the parents who face the hard truths, support our children’s growth, and help build a kinder, safer world that surely starts from home!
