Supporting Your Child to Overcome School Struggles

Ever felt unsure how to help your child when they are struggling at school? You are not alone. Surely all parents dream of seeing their children safe, healthy, and successful, hoping for the best for them, but unfortunately, some external factors may hinder this dream, one of them is bullying that they may experience. Whether a bully or a victim of bullying, supporting your child and helping them overcome their mental issues associated with bullying should be every parent’s priority.

Unfortunately, not all of us know how to handle the challenges faced by our children because of bullying, and some parents themselves may have suffered before from bullying and could not solve their problems due to the lack of guidance. However, parent education programs have the potential to help these parents become more contributing to their children's well-being!

"The most effective component of bullying prevention involves family connections such as parent psychoeducation, seminars, and information specifically about how to communicate with children and schools about bullying."
--Bradshaw

Understanding the psychological factors that drive bullying behavior is crucial. Research showed that family violence and societal factors increase the likelihood of engaging in bullying among teenagers (Al-Hussein & Shahba, 2024). Hence, family factors play a crucial role in influencing children to become bullies, as they bully to gain the appreciation and valuation they never had by showing power to others.

This imbalance stems from parents and their relation with their children because parents devalue them, or even these parents humiliate their children, creating a cycle of violence, as these children perceive aggression as the only way to force power on others, internalizing violence as a way to exert control. Therefore, bullies start to bully others, affecting their wellbeing.

The Importance of Parent Education Programs

Parent education programs help parents equip them with the needed skills to manage their children's problems, develop their empathy, and encourage them to discuss their problems with their parents. Children at a young age are not experienced enough to solve their own problems and to respond to threats with continuous humiliation or sexual activity.

They also do not fully trust their teachers because their teachers are still not part of their family, raising their concerns about whether to tell them or not as they are still strangers. So they remain silent and are bullied or start bullying others. Training parents on the different situations of bullying and how to handle them provides clear guidance for victimized children to overcome their problems.

For the bullies, parent education programs teach these parents how to develop empathy toward other peers so that they stop bullying them also these programs resolve conflicts between parents and children, building a strong relationship between them and enhancing trust and valuation, resolving the psychological problems of these children.

The Power of Parent Education in Bullying Prevention

A meta-analysis by Chen et al. (2020) revealed that parenting programs led to a significant reduction in bullying behaviors. The home-visiting programs included professionals, such as mental health and social professionals or specialized trainers who visit families to provide guidance, focusing on improving parenting practices, enhancing parent-child communication, increasing children’s empathy, and addressing specific family dynamics.

Read More


These interventions showed higher effects than school anti-bullying programs in reducing bullying behavior due to their individualized approach.

Another study by Cunha et al. (2023) shows the impact of parental involvement in empathy education on reducing bullying by highlighting the effectiveness of a low-cost, four-month parental involvement program focused on empathy education in reducing bullying. The conducted intervention in China that involved 22000 children resulted in enhanced parent-child connections and improved children's empathy levels, leading to a significant drop in both bullying perpetration and victimization.

In addition, this source ensured that it only costs $12.50 to prevent a single bullying incident, ensuring the potential of parent education programs to be cost-effective solutions to address school bullying. This indicates that parent-child connection is one of the most effective ways for bullying intervention if they are used properly.

Embracing Parent Education as a Positive Learning Experience

Some parents may perceive going to parent education programs shameful because they appear to have problems with treating their children, but actually, learning how to raise children is part of our learning experience. Even religion educates us on how to raise our children and ensure its importance, whether Islam or Christianity. So, why is this well accepted while education programs are not?

It is nature of the humans to learn. With today’s advancements, we have more knowledge than ever about effective parenting, and this saves time and effort while helping children become more productive and resilient, ready to face life's challenges correctly. Or do we just accept the fact that we should take the risk in life and let the waves of life hit us to learn? We should highlight that prevention is better than cure.

Parent education programs equip parents with the needed tools to resolve the conflicts of their children due to bullying. While school anti-bullying programs are efficient in doing so, they must be accompanied by better knowledge of parents about how to raise their children
Remember, you are not alone, and we are all here to help you be a better parent that your children will always be proud of!
